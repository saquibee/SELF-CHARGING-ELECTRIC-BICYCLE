#SELF-CHARGING-ELECTRIC-BICYCLE.
When the rider pedals, a motor would be coupled to the rear wheel. The wheel drives the Permanent Magnet DC (PMDC) motor, which operates in generator mode. This converts the mechanical energy from wheel rotation into electrical energy. The generated output is in the form of alternating or unregulated DC voltage depending on speed.
The electrical output from the generator is fed into a bridge rectifier, which converts it into a stable DC voltage suitable for further processing. This rectified DC is then supplied to a buck-boost DC–DC converter. The buck-boost converter plays a crucial role in regulating the voltage level by either stepping it up or stepping it down, ensuring that a suitable and safe charging voltage is delivered to the battery regardless of speed variations.
A blocking diode is placed after the converter to prevent reverse current flow from the battery back to the generator, thereby protecting system components and avoiding energy loss.
The regulated power is then supplied to the Battery Management System (BMS). The BMS continuously monitors battery parameters such as voltage, current, and temperature. It ensures safe charging, protects against over-charging, over-discharging, and thermal damage, and maintains voltage balance among battery cells.
Finally, the conditioned and controlled energy is stored in the lithium-ion battery pack (7S2P configuration).
